[ Materials ]

We buy the tree, not the board.

Timber is bought in the log, air-dried three years under cover, then finished in the kiln to furniture moisture. We work four materials, and we work them honestly — no veneer, no stain pretending to be another wood, no filler in the grain.

White oak

rift & quarter-sawn · Janka 1360 · 0.3%/RH

Cut on the quarter so the medullary rays flash silver across the face. The most stable timber we work — a drawer front in oak barely moves through a wet winter.

American walnut

plain-sawn · Janka 1010 · oiled, never stained

Chocolate heartwood that lightens toward honey with age and daylight. We book-match the boards so a tabletop reads as one mirrored sheet of grain, not a run of planks.

Blackened steel

3 mm hot-rolled · TIG-welded · wax-inhibited

Frames and trestles are cut from hot-rolled plate, welded, ground flush, then blackened with heat and oil rather than paint. It ages to a soft graphite, and a scratch just adds to it.

Belgian linen

480 g/m² flax · loose cushion · washable

Upholstery is heavyweight Belgian flax over latex and feather. Every cover is loose and zipped, so a spilt glass of red is a wash, not a re-upholstery.
